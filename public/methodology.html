Methodology

What This Shows

The Commodities Compass visualizes which commodities are outperforming or underperforming relative to each other. It's a snapshot of current trends, not a prediction of future prices.

How It Works

We track 20 major commodity futures across energy, metals, grains, softs, and livestock. For each commodity, we calculate:

  • Relative Strength (X-axis) — Is this commodity outperforming or underperforming the average? Uses percentile ranking over 252 trading days.
  • Momentum (Y-axis) — Is that relative performance improving or fading? Also percentile-ranked.

The Quadrants

  • Leading (top-right) — Outperforming and momentum is positive
  • Weakening (bottom-right) — Still outperforming but losing steam
  • Lagging (bottom-left) — Underperforming and momentum is negative
  • Improving (top-left) — Still underperforming but starting to turn

Commodities tend to rotate clockwise through these quadrants over time.

Term Structure

For energy and metals, we also show whether the futures curve is in backwardation (near-term prices higher than far-term, often bullish) or contango (far-term higher, often bearish for holders).

Parameters

  • RS-Ratio smoothing: 100 periods
  • RS-Momentum: 35-period rate of change
  • Percentile window: 252 trading days (~1 year)
  • Benchmark: Equal-weight geometric mean of all 20 commodities

This methodology is based on Relative Rotation Graphs (RRG), developed by Julius de Kempenaer. We use percentile ranking instead of z-scores to handle the non-normal distribution of commodity returns.

Data

Prices from Yahoo Finance. Delayed 15+ minutes. Updates every 15 minutes during market hours.

Limitations

This is a visualization tool. It shows what's happening, not what will happen. Past relative performance does not predict future returns. Use it as one input among many.
